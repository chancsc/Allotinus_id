Wing diagrams and terminology used throughout the Allotinus identification key and Feature Scoring questions.

Wing venation & space numbering

Identification keys and questions refer to numbered "spaces" (the cells between veins) rather than the veins themselves — e.g. "hindwing space 6" or "forewing vein 4". This diagram shows the numbering convention used throughout this app.

Forewing (top) and hindwing (bottom) venation

Spaces are numbered outward from the wing base, starting at the dorsum (inner margin) and running around to the costa (leading edge). Forewing spaces run 1b–12; hindwing spaces run 1a, 1b, then 2–8. A vein is numbered the same as the space immediately above it (e.g. "vein 4" runs along the upper edge of space 4).

Space The cell of wing membrane bounded by two adjacent veins. Vein The rigid supporting structure separating two spaces; named after the space below it. Costa The leading (front) edge of the wing, from the wing base to the apex. Dorsum The inner (trailing) margin of the wing, closest to the body/abdomen. Termen The outer margin of the wing, between the apex and the tornus. Postdiscal Refers to a spot or band positioned outward (toward the termen) of the discal cell.
