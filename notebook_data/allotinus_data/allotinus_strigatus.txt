Allotinus strigatus malayanus belongs to the subgenus Fabitaras. It is a common forest butterfly typically found at low or moderate elevations.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The wings are plain dark brown. A key diagnostic feature is the basal portion of forewing vein 4, which is swollen for half its length. This swelling is accompanied by a "visual brand" of paler scales that is approximately 2.0 mm wide.

Antennae: As a member of the Fabitaras subgenus, the antennae are relatively long, measuring nearly two-thirds the length of the forewing and consisting of 60 or more segments.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The underside is characteristically pale buff.

Hindwing Arrangement: The postdiscal stria in space 6 is positioned mid-way between the striae in spaces 5 and 7. Crucially, the outer postdiscal stria in space 7 is placed well inside the stria in space 6.

Submarginal Spots: It features a series of black submarginal spots on both wings that are prominently edged with white on their outer sides.

Subgeneric Features: In this subgenus, a weak precostal vein is normally present on the hindwing, and the male's abdominal hair tufts are inconspicuous.

Wing Length and Size

Size: Specific millimetric measurements for the total forewing length are not provided in the primary identification keys, but the species is figured at natural size in the reference plates. The male's visual brand on the upperside is 2.0 mm wide.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species. The hindwing is described as being rounded.
