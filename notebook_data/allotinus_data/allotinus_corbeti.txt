Allotinus corbeti (Corbet's Darkwing) is a very small species belonging to the subgenus Paragerydus.

Upperside Identification Keys

Coloration: Both the male and female are a uniform reddish brown.

Male Features: The forewing apex is rounded. Vein 4 of the forewing is swollen for one-third of its length, but unlike many other species in the genus, it lacks a "visual brand" of paler scales.

Underside Identification Keys

Marking Style: The underside pattern resembles a miniature version of Allotinus leogoron.

Subgeneric Features: As a member of the Paragerydus subgenus, it lacks a precostal vein on the hindwing. The male possesses prominent, permanently extruded hair tufts on the 8th abdominal segment.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: This is a small butterfly with a forewing length typically between 10.0 mm and 12.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species.
