Allotinus fabius arrius (The Angled Darkwing) belongs to the subgenus Fabitaras. It is primarily differentiated from its relatives by the specific blotchiness of its underside markings and its tailed hindwing.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The ground color is greyish white.

Marking Style: It features dense striations across both wings.

Distinctive Feature: The postdiscal spots are characteristically enlarged and blotchy above vein 4 on the forewing and above vein 6 on the hindwing.

Submarginal Spots: It possesses a series of black submarginal spots that are prominently edged with white on their outer sides.

Hindwing Arrangement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned either directly below or just inside (basad) the spot in space 7.

Subgeneric Features: Like other members of its subgenus, a weak precostal vein is normally present on the hindwing.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The wings are plain dark brown. A secondary sexual character is the strongly swollen basal portion of vein 4 on the forewing.

Female: In the Malay Peninsula and north Sumatra, the upperside is uniform brown. However, in populations from south Sumatra and Borneo, the female features a broad white tornal area on the hindwing.

Antennae: The antennae are nearly two-thirds the length of the forewing, typically consisting of 60 or more segments.

Wing Length and Size

Size: This species is described as being "rather smaller" than its close relative, Allotinus borneensis (which has a forewing length of 16.0–19.0 mm).

Tail Information

Tail: The hindwing is characteristically caudate (tailed) at vein 4 in the male.
