Allotinus davidis (The Black-spotted Darkwing) belongs to the subgenus Paragerydus. It is a very small species, primarily distinguished from similar-looking relatives by its ground color and specific spotting.

Upperside Identification Keys

Coloration: The upperside of the wings is brown.

Costal Feature: A characteristic white costal fleck is present at the end of vein 7 on the forewing.

Male Features: Unlike many other species in the genus, the male lacks a "visual brand" of paler scales on the forewing.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The underside is characteristically pale buff.

Marking Style: As with other Allotinus, it is densely sprinkled with dark brown dots and striae, but the pattern is often more distinct in this species.

Hindwing Spots: A key diagnostic feature is that the central spot in space 7 is blackened. Additionally, the postdiscal spot in space 7 is blackened on its inner edge.

Subgeneric Features: As a member of the Paragerydus subgenus, it lacks a precostal vein on the hindwing. The male possesses prominent, permanently extruded hair tufts on the 8th abdominal segment.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: This is a small butterfly, with a forewing length typically ranging from 9.0 mm to 14.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species.
