Allotinus unicolor unicolor (The Lesser Darkwing) belongs to the subgenus Paragerydus. It is a common butterfly found in lowland jungle habitats.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The wings are dark brown. A key diagnostic feature is a narrow, short "visual brand" of paler scales located astride the swollen portion of vein 4 on the forewing.

Female: The wings are reddish brown. Compared to the male, the forewing is less pointed and the hindwing is more dentately sinuate.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: Typically greyish white, though it can vary to a darker grey.

Marking Style: Dense sprinkles of dark brown dots and striae form an obscure pattern. In typical specimens, the principal markings are very well defined.

Postdiscal Band: On the forewing, the postdiscal series of spots is arranged on a more or less regular curve.

Hindwing Arrangement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned well inside the outer spot in space 7.

Subbasal Features: Unlike some other subgenera, it lacks a precostal vein on the hindwing.

Size and Structure

Wing Length: This species varies greatly in size. In males, the visual brand on the forewing is narrow and covers only half the length of vein 4.

Tail: It is a tailless species. However, the female's hindwing has a dentately sinuate (toothed) appearance.

Secondary Sexual Characters: In the male, the basal portion of forewing vein 4 is swollen. Additionally, the hair tufts on the 8th abdominal segment are prominent and permanently extruded.
