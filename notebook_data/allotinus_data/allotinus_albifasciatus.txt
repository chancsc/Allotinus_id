Allotinus albifasciatus (The White-banded Darkwing) belongs to the nominate subgenus Allotinus. It is recognized as the rarest of all the Malaysian Allotinus species.

Upperside Identification Keys

Forewing: Features distinct white areas.

Hindwing: Characteristically marked with a broad white band.

Vein Structure: In the male, vein 4 of the forewing is not, or only very weakly, swollen. Additionally, veins 6 and 7 of the forewing are separate.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: Typically greyish-white.

Pattern Style: The underside is densely sprinkled with dark brown dots and striae, forming an obscure pattern.

Differentiation: Compared to the related Allotinus fallax apus, the underside of A. albifasciatus is whiter and the markings are somewhat blotchier.

Subgeneric Features: In this subgenus, a weak precostal vein is normally present on the hindwing.

Size and Structure

Wing Length: Specific forewing length measurements are not explicitly provided in the primary identification keys for this species. 

Tail: It is a tailless species.

Antennae: Barely longer than half the forewing, with fewer than 50 segments.

Habitat and Distribution

Elevation: It is recorded at elevations of 750 meters and upwards.

Location: Found in the Malay Peninsula and north Sumatra.
