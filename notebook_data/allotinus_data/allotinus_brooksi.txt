Allotinus brooksi (The White-tornal Darkwing) belongs to the subgenus Fabitaras. It is a Bornean endemic species primarily recorded from Bau, Sarawak.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The ground color of the underside is whitish.

Central Marking: A key diagnostic feature of the underside is a distinct dark spot located in the middle of the wing.

Subgeneric Features: As a member of the subgenus Fabitaras, it typically possesses a weak precostal vein on the hindwing.

Upperside Identification Keys

Hindwing: The most prominent identification feature on the upperside is the tornal area (or tornal quarter) of the hindwing, which is white.

Antennae: In this subgenus, the antennae are nearly two-thirds the length of the forewing, typically consisting of 60 or more segments.

Wing Length and Size

Size: The forewing length for this species is approximately 15.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species.
