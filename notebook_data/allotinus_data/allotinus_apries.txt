Allotinus apries apries belongs to the subgenus Paragerydus. It is primarily distinguished from its relatives by its smooth hindwing margins and specific wing markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal series of spots is parallel to the wing margin (termen) and is typically rather obscure.

Hindwing Style: Unlike many other members of the genus, the hindwing is not dentate (the edges are smooth and not toothed). The overall markings on the underside are considered ill-defined compared to similar species.

Subgeneric Features: As a member of Paragerydus, it lacks a precostal vein on the hindwing.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The wings are dark brown. The forewing features a broad, oval "visual brand" of paler scales that extends more than half the length of vein 4. The basal portion of forewing vein 4 is swollen.

Female: The wings are dark brown. It is identified by a characteristic paler area on the forewing located beyond the end of the cell.

Wing Length and Size

Size: This species is categorized as "larger" in the identification keys, specifically larger than the related Allotinus corbeti, which has a forewing length of 10.0–12.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species. Its hindwing is notably smooth and lacks the dentations (tooth-like projections) found in species such as Allotinus unicolor or Allotinus leogoron.
