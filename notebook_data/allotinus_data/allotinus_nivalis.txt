Allotinus nivalis belongs to the subgenus Paragerydus. It is a species primarily associated with Borneo.

Upperside Identification Keys

Coloration: The upperside of the wings is brown.

Diagnostic Feature: A characteristic white costal fleck is present at the end of vein 7 on the forewing.

Male Features: Unlike many other members of the genus, the male of this species lacks a "visual brand" of paler scales on the forewing.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Spotting: A key diagnostic feature to differentiate it from similar small species is that the central spot in space 7 on the hindwing is not blackened. 

Subgeneric Features: As a member of the Paragerydus subgenus, it lacks a precostal vein on the hindwing. The male possesses prominent, permanently extruded hair tufts on the 8th abdominal segment.

Wing Length and Size

Size: It is categorized as a "larger" species within its subgroup.

Forewing Length: The forewing length typically ranges from 12.5 mm to 15.5 mm.

Tail Information
