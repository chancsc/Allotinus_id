Allotinus otsukai (The Bornean White-wing) is a Bornean endemic belonging to the nominate subgenus Allotinus. It is primarily found in the Gunung Kinabalu area and Mamut.

Upperside Identification Keys

Forewing: The upperside features a sullied white area that extends all the way to the wing base. The costal and distal margins are dark brown.

Hindwing: Plain dark brown without any white or blue markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Pattern Style: As a member of the subgenus Allotinus, the underside is greyish-white and densely sprinkled with dark brown dots and striae. These markings form the obscure patterns of cellular, postdiscal, and submarginal spots common to the genus.

Subgeneric Features: In this nominate subgenus, a weak precostal vein is normally present on the hindwing.

Wing Length and Size

Male: The forewing length is 15.0 mm.

Female: The forewing length typically ranges between 14.0 mm and 15.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species. The hindwing margins are regular and do not feature the dentations seen in some other subgenera.
