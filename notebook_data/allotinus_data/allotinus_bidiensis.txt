Allotinus bidiensis belongs to the subgenus Fabitaras. It is a Bornean species recorded from the Gunung Kinabalu area, Keningau, and Sarawak.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The underside is white, densely sprinkled with dark brown scales and striae.

Hindwing Arrangement: The postdiscal stria in space 6 is placed well outside (distad) the stria in space 7.

Subgeneric Features: In this subgenus, a weak precostal vein is normally present on the hindwing.

Male Secondary Characters: On the forewing, vein 4 is swollen for approximately one-third of its length. The associated "visual brand" of paler scales is inconspicuous and less than 1.0 mm wide.

Upperside Identification Keys

Wings: The upperside of both the forewings and hindwings is plain dark brown.

Antennae: Members of the subgenus Fabitaras have antennae that are nearly two-thirds the length of the forewing, typically consisting of 60 or more segments.

Wing Length and Size

Male: The forewing length is typically between 15.0 mm and 16.0 mm.

Female: The forewing length is approximately 14.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species. The hindwing is described as being rounded rather than dentate or produced.
