Allotinus substrigosus substrigosus belongs to the subgenus Paragerydus. It is a very small species typically found in lowland jungle habitats.

Upperside Identification Keys

Coloration: The wings are brown.

Diagnostic Feature: A characteristic white costal fleck is present at the end of vein 7 on the forewing.

Male Secondary Characters: Unlike many other members of the genus, the male of this species lacks a "visual brand" of paler scales on the forewing.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The ground color is characteristically whitish.

Marking Style: Densely sprinkled with dark brown dots and striae that form an obscure pattern of spots.

Distinctive Feature: It is most easily recognized by a prominent blackish spot located below the center of the hindwing costa.

Differentiation: It can be distinguished from the closely related Allotinus davidis by the fact that the postdiscal spot in space 7 on the hindwing is not blackened.

Subgeneric Features: As a member of the Paragerydus group, it lacks a precostal vein on the hindwing. The male possesses prominent, permanently extruded hair tufts on the 8th abdominal segment.

Wing Length and Size

Size: This species is categorized as very small.

Forewing Length: The forewing length typically ranges from 9.0 mm to 14.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species.
