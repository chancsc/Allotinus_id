Allotinus fallax apus (The Common Darkwing) belongs to the nominate subgenus Allotinus. It is an uncommon butterfly found in lowland forest areas.

Upperside Identification Keys

Coloration: The forewing is white with broad brown borders. The hindwing is plain brown.

Male Features: Vein 4 of the forewing is not, or only very slightly, swollen. Additionally, forewing veins 6 and 7 are separate (not stalked).

Subgeneric Traits: The antennae are short, barely longer than half the length of the forewing, and consist of fewer than 50 segments.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: Typically white or greyish.

Pattern Style: The underside is densely sprinkled with dark brown dots and striae. These markings form an obscure pattern of cellular, postdiscal, and submarginal spots common to the genus.

Comparison: Compared to the related Allotinus albifasciatus, the underside of A. fallax apus is less white and the markings are less blotchy.

Subgeneric Traits: A weak precostal vein is normally present on the hindwing.

Wing Length and Size

Size: Specific forewing length measurements are not provided in the primary Malayan identification keys for this species. It is noted as being part of a subgenus with relatively short antennae compared to the wing length.

Tail Information

Tail: It is a tailless species. The hindwing margins are generally smooth compared to the dentate (toothed) margins found in other subgenera like Paragerydus.
